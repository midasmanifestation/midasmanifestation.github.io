Getting Started: What to Do First

Before you press play on anything, take 10 minutes to set yourself up properly. I have seen people buy the program, skip straight to Track 5 because the name sounded exciting, and then wonder why nothing happened. The order matters. The sequence matters. Here is what to do first.

  1. Download all 5 audio tracks to your phone or computer. The MP3 files are available immediately in your member area after purchase. Do not stream them — download them so you have zero dependency on internet connectivity during sessions.
  2. Read the Quick Start Guide. This is a 4-page PDF that explains the listening schedule, session duration, and setup. It takes about 5 minutes to read and saves you from the most common beginner mistakes.
  3. Grab headphones. The audio tracks contain binaural beats that work best when delivered separately to each ear. Any over-ear or in-ear headphones will do. If you do not have headphones, speakers work too — just at a lower intensity.
  4. Find your listening spot. Choose a place where you will not be interrupted for 15-30 minutes. A bedroom, a parked car during lunch, a quiet office — anywhere you can close your eyes and focus.
  5. Set a consistent time. Morning within 30 minutes of waking is ideal because your brain is still in a theta/alpha state. Evening before bed works almost as well. The key is consistency, not perfection.

That is it. You do not need to read the full 118-page e-book before starting. You do not need to buy anything extra. You do not need to meditate for an hour or change your entire lifestyle. Just download, read the quick start, put on headphones, and press play on Track 1.

Common Mistakes to Avoid

I have reviewed hundreds of user experiences with the Midas Manifestation system. The people who get results do a few things differently from those who quit after two weeks. Here are the 5 most common mistakes I see, along with the fix for each one.

Mistake 01
Skipping Tracks Because They "Sound Boring"
Some users listen to Track 1, decide it sounds like ambient noise, and skip to Track 5 because the name "Midas Unleashed" sounds more exciting. This is like trying to run a marathon without stretching first. The sequence exists for a reason.
Fix: Trust the sequence. Track 1 opens your third eye so you can recognize opportunities. Track 2 opens your crown so guidance can reach you. Without these, Tracks 3-5 have a harder time producing visible results. Give each track its full 3-day minimum.
Mistake 02
Listening Without Headphones
The binaural beats in the audio tracks work by playing slightly different frequencies in each ear. When you listen through speakers, both ears hear the same thing, which eliminates the binaural effect. You still get the isochronic tones, but you miss a significant portion of the technology.
Fix: Use any headphones, even basic earbuds. You do not need expensive over-ear cans. If you absolutely cannot use headphones, turn the volume up slightly and sit in a quiet room to minimize external noise interference.
Mistake 03
Expecting Overnight Results
Some users listen for 3 days, see no change in their bank account, and declare the program a scam. The Midas Manifestation system works on your subconscious mind and energy field — not on your bank account directly. The financial results are a downstream effect of internal shifts.
Fix: Commit to the full 30 days before evaluating. Track your internal shifts (mood, confidence, sleep quality, decision-making speed) instead of only tracking external money events. The internal shifts come first. The external results follow.
Mistake 04
Inconsistent Listening Schedule
Listening on Monday, skipping Tuesday and Wednesday, then listening again on Thursday does not produce the same results as consistent daily listening. The frequency programming works through repetition and accumulation. Irregular exposure resets the process.
Fix: Set a daily alarm for your listening session. Tie it to an existing habit (right after brushing your teeth, before your morning coffee, during your lunch break). Missing one day is fine. Missing three or more in a row means you should restart that track from day 1.
Mistake 05
Ignoring the Journaling Prompts
The audio tracks do the heavy lifting, but the journaling accelerates the process. Writing forces your conscious mind to process what your subconscious is releasing. Skipping journaling is like going to therapy but refusing to talk about anything.
Fix: Keep it simple. You do not need a fancy journal or a 30-minute writing session. Spend 5 minutes writing about what you noticed during or after your listening session. Even bullet points count. The act of writing matters more than the length.
