Solar Plexus Chakra for Wealth: Boost Confidence and Financial Power

What Is the Solar Plexus Chakra?

The solar plexus chakra sits in your upper abdomen, right between your ribcage and your navel. In Sanskrit, it is called Manipura, which translates to "city of jewels." Its color is yellow. Its element is fire. And its job is to make you feel like you are in charge of your own life.

I spent years studying the chakra system before I understood how directly the solar plexus impacts your bank account. Most people think money problems come from not earning enough or spending too much. Those are surface symptoms. The root cause — the one that actually determines your income ceiling — sits in the fire center of your body.

Manipura controls your sense of personal power. It governs how confident you feel making decisions, how strongly you advocate for yourself, and whether you believe you are the kind of person who deserves abundance. When this chakra is firing on all cylinders, you walk into salary negotiations like you own the room. When it is blocked, you undercharge, avoid conversations about money, and quietly settle for less than you are worth.

The physical location matters too. The solar plexus sits near your stomach, liver, and adrenal glands — all organs involved in digestion, energy production, and stress response. A blocked solar plexus does not just affect your mindset. It creates physical tension, digestive issues, and chronic fatigue that drains the energy you need to build wealth.

How the Solar Plexus Affects Your Money

Most people treat money as a math problem. Budget better, spend less, invest more. But anyone who has tried those steps and still felt stuck knows the math is not the issue. The issue is how you feel about yourself in relation to money. That is Manipura territory.

Personal power. The solar plexus is the seat of your willpower. When it is strong, you take action on financial goals. You follow through. You do the uncomfortable thing — sending that invoice, making that sales call, raising your rates. When it is weak, you procrastinate on every money-related task that requires courage. You know what to do, but you cannot make yourself do it.

Confidence to earn. Earning money requires you to believe you have something valuable to offer. The solar plexus is where that belief lives. I have worked with freelancers who produce excellent work but charge half of what they should because they cannot shake the feeling that they are "not that good." That is a solar plexus problem, not a skills problem.

Worthiness. There is a difference between knowing you deserve more money and feeling it in your body. The solar plexus is where intellectual knowledge becomes felt conviction. You can repeat affirmations all day, but if your Manipura is blocked, the words bounce off. When it opens, you stop apologizing for your rates. You stop shrinking to make other people comfortable. You feel worthy, and your financial behavior follows.

Decision-making. Financial success is built on hundreds of small decisions — when to invest, when to save, when to take a risk, when to walk away. The solar plexus governs your ability to decide quickly and trust yourself. A blocked Manipura creates paralysis. You overthink every financial choice, second-guess yourself constantly, and miss opportunities because you could not pull the trigger.

Signs Your Solar Plexus Chakra Is Blocked

A blocked solar plexus does not announce itself. It hides behind what looks like practical behavior. You think you are being "realistic" or "careful," but the real driver is fear disguised as logic. Here are the most common signs that show up around money.

Undercharging for your work. You set your prices based on what feels "safe" rather than what your work is actually worth. You lower your rates to match competitors instead of standing behind your value. You give discounts before anyone asks. The pattern is always the same — you shrink your income to avoid the discomfort of being seen as expensive.

Fear of asking for raises. You have not brought up compensation in months — maybe years. You rehearse the conversation in your head but never have it. When the topic comes up, you downplay your contributions. This is not humility. It is a blocked solar plexus telling your nervous system that asserting your value is dangerous.

Imposter syndrome. You feel like a fraud no matter how much evidence proves otherwise. You land a client and wonder why they chose you. You hit a revenue milestone and attribute it to luck. The solar plexus is supposed to give you the quiet confidence that says, "I earned this." When it is blocked, that voice is replaced by "Who are you to charge that much?"

Procrastination on money tasks. You put off invoicing. You delay following up with clients. You avoid looking at your numbers. You start a business plan and abandon it halfway through. The tasks themselves are not difficult — your solar plexus is not generating enough fire energy to push through the discomfort of doing them.

Saying yes to everything. A blocked solar plexus makes it nearly impossible to set boundaries. You take on low-paying projects because you cannot bring yourself to say no. You overwork to prove your value instead of charging appropriately. You become the person who does everything for everyone — except make money for yourself.

If three or more of these descriptions fit you, your solar plexus needs attention. I recognize these patterns because I lived them for years. The fix is not more hustle or better strategy. It is clearing the energy blockage that makes those things feel impossible.

It governs your sense of security, your relationship with basic needs, and whether your nervous system feels safe enough to handle money calmly. A blocked root chakra creates money anxiety, hoarding, panic spending, and an inability to feel secure regardless of your account balance. It is the foundation — without it, everything else crumbles.

The solar plexus chakra (Manipura) is about financial power and confidence. It governs your self-worth, your ability to earn, and your willingness to take bold action on money. A blocked solar plexus shows up as undercharging, imposter syndrome, procrastination, and chronic self-doubt about your earning potential. Here is a practical way to think about it. The root chakra keeps you from panicking about money. The solar plexus gives you the drive to make more of it. If your root chakra is blocked, you feel anxious about finances even when things are going well. If your solar plexus is blocked, you feel stuck and unable to move forward even when you are not actively struggling.
