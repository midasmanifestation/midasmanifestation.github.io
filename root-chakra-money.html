Root Chakra and Money: How to Heal Financial Blockages for Good

If you struggle with money no matter how hard you work, your root chakra might be the missing piece. Here is what the root chakra actually governs, why it directly impacts your finances, and how to clear the blocks that keep you stuck in scarcity.

Published June 23, 2026 12 min read

What Is the Root Chakra?

The root chakra sits at the base of your spine. In Sanskrit, it is called Muladhara, which literally translates to "root support." Its color is red. Its element is earth. And its job is straightforward: keep you alive, safe, and stable.

When I first learned about the seven chakras years ago, I thought they were just spiritual concepts with no real-world impact. But the root chakra is different. It governs your survival instincts, your physical health, your sense of safety in the world, and — here is where it gets interesting — your relationship with money.

Think about it. Money is how modern humans secure food, shelter, clothing, and safety. It is the tool we use to meet every survival need. So when your root chakra is blocked or underactive, your nervous system does not feel safe. And when your nervous system does not feel safe, money becomes a source of constant anxiety — no matter how much you actually have.

The root chakra also connects to your adrenal glands, which produce cortisol — the stress hormone. A blocked root chakra keeps your body in fight-or-flight mode. You feel rushed, panicked, and reactive. Financial decisions made from this state are almost always poor ones. You either avoid money entirely or obsess over it in unhealthy ways.

How a Blocked Root Chakra Shows Up Around Money

Most people do not realize their financial patterns are rooted in energy blockages. They think it is a discipline problem or a knowledge problem. But I have talked to people earning six figures who still feel broke, and people earning modest incomes who feel genuinely abundant. The difference is rarely the number in their bank account. It is the state of their root chakra.

Chronic money anxiety. This is the most common sign. You check your bank account five times a day. You lose sleep over bills even when they are paid. You feel a constant low-grade panic about money that never goes away, regardless of your actual financial situation. Your nervous system is stuck in survival mode, and your root chakra is not providing the grounding it should.

Hoarding or overspending. Both behaviors come from the same root cause: a lack of safety. Hoarders save compulsively because they believe disaster is always around the corner. Overspenders drain their accounts because the temporary rush of buying something numbs the anxiety. Neither pattern is about the money itself. Both are about a root chakra that cannot feel secure.

Undercharging for your work. If you are a freelancer, business owner, or service provider, a blocked root chakra can make you price yourself far below your value. You settle for less because deep down, you do not believe you deserve more — or you fear that asking for more will drive people away. This is a survival response, not a business strategy.

Money avoidance. Some people simply refuse to look at their finances. They do not open bank statements. They avoid budgeting. They let debts pile up without addressing them. This is not laziness. It is a root chakra defense mechanism. The body is protecting you from a perceived threat by making you freeze.

Self-sabotage. You get a raise, and then your car breaks down. You land a great client, and then you miss a deadline. You build savings, and then an impulse purchase drains it. When your root chakra is blocked, you unconsciously destroy progress because success does not feel safe. Your system is wired to return to what is familiar — even if what is familiar is financial struggle.

If you recognize three or more of these patterns in yourself, your root chakra needs attention. The good news: it can be healed. And when it heals, your entire relationship with money shifts — often faster than you expect.

The Root Chakra-Money Connection

Here is what most people miss about the root chakra and money: it is not just about spiritual energy. There is a real physiological connection. Your root chakra sits near the coccyx, which is connected to the psoas muscle — the deepest core muscle in your body. The psoas is directly linked to your fight-or-flight response. When it is tight and chronically activated (which happens with a blocked root chakra), your body stays in stress mode.

Stress mode does terrible things to your finances. Research from the American Psychological Association shows that financial stress is the number one cause of anxiety in the United States. But here is the twist: the stress often comes before the financial problem. Your nervous system creates the anxiety, and then your anxious decisions create the financial problems. It is a loop.

Childhood money beliefs get stored here too. If you grew up hearing "money doesn't grow on trees" or "we can't afford that" or watched your parents fight about bills, those experiences imprinted on your root chakra. Your six-year-old self decided that money equals danger, and that belief has been running in the background ever since — without your awareness.

I spent years wondering why I could not break through a certain income ceiling. I read every personal finance book. I tracked every dollar. I automated my savings. Nothing stuck. Then someone explained the root chakra-money connection to me, and it clicked. My nervous system was not wired for abundance. It was wired for survival. I had to rewire it from the bottom up.

This is why budgeting apps and saving tips alone do not work for everyone. They address the surface behavior, but they do not touch the root cause — which is, literally, the root chakra.

The earth carries a natural electrical charge. Research from the Journal of Environmental and Public Health found that grounding reduces cortisol levels, improves sleep, and lowers stress — all of which directly support root chakra healing.

I started grounding every morning for 15 minutes before checking my phone. Within two weeks, I noticed I was less reactive about money decisions. The panic that used to hit when I looked at my bank account started to fade. It was not magic. It was my nervous system finally calming down.

Root Chakra Affirmations

Affirmations work when they are specific and repeated consistently. Generic statements like "I am abundant" do not touch the root chakra because they are too vague. Instead, use affirmations that speak directly to survival and safety:

  • "I am safe. I am supported. I have enough."

  • "My body is relaxed. My finances are stable. I am grounded."
  • "I release all fear about money. I trust that I am provided for."

Say these out loud while standing or sitting with your feet flat on the floor. The physical posture matters — it signals safety to your nervous system. I repeat mine every morning while making coffee. Takes 30 seconds. The cumulative effect over weeks is real.
