Background

What Is The Secret?

The Secret is a 2006 book and film created by Rhonda Byrne. The book has sold over 35 million copies and has been translated into more than 50 languages. It is probably the most well-known manifestation resource ever created.

The core idea behind The Secret is the law of attraction — the belief that your thoughts attract corresponding experiences into your life. Think positive, get positive results. Think negative, get negative results. Byrne argues that this is a universal law, similar to gravity, that has been known by successful people throughout history but kept hidden from the masses.

The book features interviews with 24 "teachers" including Bob Proctor, Jack Canfield, Lisa Nichols, and Joe Vitale. Each contributor shares their perspective on how thoughts shape reality. The tone is inspirational and motivational. Byrne weaves together stories, anecdotes, and spiritual concepts into a narrative about the power of your mind.

The Secret is fundamentally a philosophy book. It explains the concept behind manifestation, tells stories of people who have used it, and encourages you to change your thinking patterns. There is no structured daily program. No audio tracks. No step-by-step schedule. You read it, absorb the ideas, and then figure out your own practice.

The DVD version presents the same material in a documentary format. The film version is similar. There is also a mobile app, but it is primarily a companion to the book rather than a standalone program. The Secret has expanded into a whole brand with sequels like The Power, The Magic, and Hero, each exploring different aspects of the original concept.

For many people, The Secret was their first introduction to manifestation. It opened the door to an entirely new way of thinking about reality. But after reading it, a common question emerges: "Okay, I understand the concept now. But what do I actually do every day?" That is where programs like Midas Manifestation come in.
