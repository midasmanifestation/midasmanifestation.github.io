What Is Midas Manifestation?

Midas Manifestation is a digital program built around the idea that specific sound frequencies, applied to specific energy centers in your body, can change how you think and feel about money. The creator, Vincent Smith, is a linguist and cultural researcher who spent over two decades studying the relationship between sound, consciousness, and material reality.

The system comes with 5 audio tracks, each targeting a different chakra with a specific solfeggio frequency. You start with the third eye activation track (288 Hz), move through the crown chakra (216 Hz), heart chakra (639 Hz), solar plexus (528 Hz), and finish with the root chakra (369 Hz). The logic is that each track opens an energy channel so the next one works deeper.

Alongside the audio, you get a 118-page illustrated e-book that explains the history behind these frequencies, the science of brainwave entrainment, and exactly how to use the program day by day. There is also a quick start guide that cuts through the theory and gives you a simple 21-day listening schedule.

At $37 for the complete package, it includes two bonus materials: a 5-module hypnosis audio course (valued at $49) and a money manifestation meditation (valued at $39). Everything downloads instantly after purchase through DigiStore24.

What Is Billionaire Brain Wave?

Billionaire Brain Wave takes a different angle on the same basic goal: rewiring your subconscious beliefs about money. Instead of mapping frequencies to chakras, this program focuses on theta brainwave entrainment. The creator, who goes by Brahm, frames the approach in more neuroscience-oriented terms rather than spiritual language.

The core idea is that theta brainwaves (the state between waking and sleep) are where your subconscious mind is most receptive to new programming. By listening to specific audio tracks that entrain your brain into theta states, you can supposedly install new beliefs about wealth and abundance directly into your subconscious. The program includes 7 audio tracks, each targeting a different aspect of money belief — things like releasing money guilt, building wealth confidence, and aligning your subconscious with abundance.

What I noticed about Billionaire Brain Wave compared to Midas Manifestation is that it leans harder into the science angle. The sales page references brainwave research, subconscious programming studies, and neuroscience concepts. Whether that makes it more credible depends on your perspective. Some people find the theta wave framing easier to accept than chakra activation. Others find the neuroscience jargon just as faith-based as the spiritual language.

The program costs around $39 and also comes with a 60-day money-back guarantee. It includes guides alongside the audio tracks, though the written material is less extensive than Midas Manifestation's 118-page e-book.

Approach Comparison: Chakra vs Brainwave

This is where the two programs diverge the most. Midas Manifestation uses the chakra system as its framework. It identifies 12 sacred chakras (going beyond the commonly known 7) and maps specific solfeggio frequencies to each one. The spiritual language is baked into every track name, every guide, and the overall philosophy.

Billionaire Brain Wave skips the spiritual framing entirely. It talks about theta states, subconscious reprogramming, and neural pathways. The audio tracks are designed to produce specific brainwave patterns rather than activate energy centers.

Here is what I actually think after testing both approaches: the underlying mechanism is probably similar regardless of the label. Both programs use audio to shift your brainwave state during listening sessions. Both create a meditative, focused window where your subconscious is more open to new ideas about money. Whether you call that "opening the root chakra" or "entraining theta waves" may not matter as much as the practical outcome.

The real question is which framing resonates with you personally. If you are comfortable with spiritual concepts and the chakra system feels intuitive, Midas Manifestation will feel natural. If you prefer something that sounds more clinical and evidence-based, Billionaire Brain Wave might be easier to take seriously.

I found that the chakra approach in Midas Manifestation gave each track a specific purpose and emotional weight that I connected with. Track 3 (heart chakra at 639 Hz) hit something deep for me — I felt a physical release in my chest that I associate with old financial anxiety clearing out. That emotional specificity is something the theta-focused approach of Billionaire Brain Wave did not produce in the same way. But that is my experience. Yours could be completely different.

User Experience: Ease of Use and Daily Commitment

Both programs are straightforward to use. You buy, download, and start listening. No special apps, no memberships to maintain, no recurring fees. The files are standard MP3 and PDF formats that work on any device.

The daily time commitment differs slightly. Midas Manifestation recommends 15-30 minutes per session. If you listen to all 5 tracks in sequence, you are looking at about 25-30 minutes. Many users listen to 1-2 tracks per day during the initial 21-day cycle, which brings it down to 15-20 minutes. Billionaire Brain Wave tracks are shorter, around 10-15 minutes each, so a single track session fits easily into a lunch break or morning routine.

For mobile access, both programs work fine. You download the files to your phone and listen through earbuds or Bluetooth headphones. No internet connection needed after the initial download.

Headphones are recommended for both programs because they use binaural beats, which require slightly different frequencies in each ear to produce the desired brainwave effect. That said, you can still benefit from speakers, particularly with isochronic tones (which Midas Manifestation includes alongside binaural beats).

In terms of structure, Midas Manifestation has a more guided progression. The 21-day cycle tells you exactly which track to listen to on which day. Billionaire Brain Wave is more flexible — you can pick tracks based on which money block you want to address. Some people prefer the structure. Others prefer the flexibility. Neither approach is objectively better; it depends on whether you want a roadmap or a menu.
